Camel

Cool facts about Camels:

  • There are two types of camels: dromedary camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • They have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Chameleon

Cool facts about Chameleons:

  • Chameleons change their colour because of their mood, changes in light or temperature, or the humidity of their environment.
  • They can move their eyes separately, with each eye turning 180 degrees.
  • Chameleon eggs can take between 4 and 12 months to hatch.

Cicada

Cool facts about Cicadas:

  • Periodical cicadas live underground for 13 or 17 years and emerge in broods.
  • Cicadas live on all continents except Antarctica.
  • Their wings are water and bacteria proof.

Panda

Cool facts about Pandas:

  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day eating, mainly on bamboo.
  • Pandas are 99% vegetarian, but for the 1% of their diet, pandas eat eggs, small animals, and etc.
  • They need at least 2 different bamboo species in their range to avoid starvation.

Pigeon

Cool facts about Pigeons:

  • They can recognise themselves and also recognise each letter of the alphabet, differentiate between photographs, and distinguish different humans within a photograph.
  • Pigeons can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans can.
  • Pigeons can fly up to 6000 feet and beyond, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.

Teddy Bear

Cool facts about Teddy Bears:

  • The first teddy bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • Washable teddy bears didn't appear until 1954.
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss.
